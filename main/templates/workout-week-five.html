Week Five

Day One - Double Circuit

Warm-Up


You will need a 20 yard space for this warm-up. Place two markers down. You will be using these markers to complete the exersizes.


If you do not have a short breath and sweat at the end of the warm up, do it again because you didn't do it right.


  1. Run to the marker and back 4 times no breaks. Effort per down and back 50%, 65%, 75%, 100%
  2. Toe Walks - Walk on your toes to the marker and back
  3. Heel Walks - Walk on your heels there and back
  4. Knee Hugs or Knee to Chest Walks, keep your shoulder blades back and traps down.
  5. Walking Quad Stretch, press through your toes, and reach to the sky with your opposite arm, really stretch your quads.
  6. Inverted Hamstring Stretch - Start off standing upright with shoulders back and chest up & out. Lift your right foot slightly off the floor. Without changing the angle of your left knee, bend your hips and lower your torso as far as you can. As you bend over, lift up your arms straight out to your sides until they are in line with your torso. Turn your palms to face up. Return back to the starting position.
  7. Rotational Lunge - Lunge forward, keep your front knee above your heel, and twist towards your up leg. Really get a hip flexor, and back stretch.
  8. Lateral Lunge - Take a medium size step with your right leg and keep your right foot facing forward. Slowly lower your hips in line with the right knee. Getting low, keep your hips back and holding this position for one to two seconds
  9. Inchworm - Keeping your legs locked, and torso flat, plant your feet and walk out to the push up position with your hands, do two pushups then walk forward with your feet, keeping your knees straight and back flat
  10. High Knees to the marker and back
  11. 5 squat hops in place, sprint through the marker.

Workout


This Week will start with a challenge. When we get back on the ice Tim and I want to know your scores. Get a solid warm up in, loosen your muscles with a dynamic workout, and get your heart rate up.

Part One: Speed

For each of these tests, complete it 5 times and take your best score.

  1. 10 Yard Sprint
  2. 20 Yard Sprint
  3. Shuttle Run - Marker at 5 yards, 15 yards, 20 yards. Sprint 15 to 5 to 20.
  4. Consecutive Cartwheels into a flip. You fail when you break form, or pause, or don’t land the flip. (Just kidding…skip this)
  5. Speed Box Test: Create a box, each line should be about 3ft long. In the center place a “X”. Clock starts when you start. Jump to the x, jump left, back to x, to the top, back to x, to right, back to x, back to start. Feet together, shoulders square to the top at all times. If you do not land on x, or do not go over the box lines you fail your attempt and will need to restart.

Part Two: Strength
  1. Max Push Ups: Go at a consistent “One-One Thousand” pace. You must fully go down to 90, and all the way back to full extension. Keep your body flat/level. If your form fails or you fall off pace you fail out.
  2. Max Sit Ups: Keep your feet flat, hands behind your head, bring your chest all the way to your knees. You must not pause. If you pause, or fail to bring your chest all the way up, or move your hands you fail.
  3. Max Dips: Find a sturdy platform that is high enough off the ground that when your arms are planted and at 90 your butt is off the ground. Legs should be straight out, and toes up. Go at a “One One-Thousand” pace. If you do not extend fully to the top, or all the way to 90, move your feet, or you pause you fail.
  4. Max Wall Sit: Feet shoulder width and flat. Back flat on the wall, shoulders on the wall, lower back on the wall. Hands straight out in front of you, knees bent to 90. If your back comes off the wall, you rest your arms on your legs, move your feet, or go above/below 90 you fail.

Cool Down


Cool Downs prevent injury, and allow your body to recover faster. It also is a great opportunity to really work on your flexibility.


Each stretch you should hold for no less than 30 seconds.


Make sure you stretch out your Calves, Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings, Groin, Hip-Flexiors, Back, Shoulders, and Neck. Really work of your flexibility, and take long slow breathes.


It's a really good idea to drink 2 bottles of water, a gatorade, and a protein shake after any workout. Your body needs hydration, electrolytes, protein, sugars, vitamins, and carbohydrates to properly recover post workout. Nutritional timing is 30-60 minutes after any workout. Make sure you're taking care of your diet, and putting in what it needs to increase your optimum fitness.

Week Five

Day Two - Hungriest Dog Runs The Fastest


Today we’re going to shock your system. Don’t worry, pain is only temporary and it leads to growth. Make sure you get a good warm up in and get your heart rate up. For these exercises draw out a line about an inch wide, or use an old hockey stick. complete each one in 3 sets, with 90 seconds per set. Do NOT pace yourself. Get from 1st to 6th gear by the end of the block and keep the pedal down. Be out of your comfort zone, but stay in control. You don't want to lose composure. Great athletes look unphased and calm while under pressure and giving it their all.


Part One
  1. Speed Hops - Over and back.
  2. Speed Hops - Side to side.
  3. Speed Hops - Over and back Right leg only.
  4. Speed Hops - Over and back Left leg only
  5. Speed Hops - Side to side right leg only
  6. Speed Hops - Side to side left leg only

Part Two

For these exercises place a marker at the starting line, and another at 20 yards. Complete each exercise in 3 sets (down and back = 1)

  1. Lunges with a hop
  2. Squat Hops
  3. Bear Crawl
  4. 10 Mountain climbers into a sprint
  5. 10 Squat hops into a sprint
  6. From the lunging position, opened up to the inside, press off your front leg into a sprint. Alternate sides (3 sets each side)
  7. From the lunging position, opened up to the outside, cross over your front leg and sprint. Alternate side (3 sets each side)
  8. Sprint

Week Five

Day Three - Welcome to the jungle


Get a strong warm up in. You did this last week. Try to improve your form. Your muscles may be sore from the past two days, today should loosen you back up.

Circuit One

  1. Monkey Runs Down and Back 3x (Face the same way). Keep your chest up, knees bent to 90, arms above your head like a monkey. You’re going to forward/lateral Cross Over. No steps in between, just constant one way cross overs through the line. Do this as fast as you can! Heres a video of a monkey if you don’t understand my description: Monkey Walk
  2. Bear Crawl Down and Back 3x. Get on all fours and run! as fast as you can.
  3. Scorpions 20x each way. Lay on your stomach with your arms stretched out straight from your shoulders. Bring your opposite leg to opposite hand while keeping your other leg straight, and your arms/shoulders down. Do these with pace but with control.
  4. Duck Walks Down and Back 3x. Chest up, Butt to heels (it should touch lightly), Walk forward, DO NOT BRING YOUR LEGS TO THE SIDE KEEP THEM MOVING IN A STRAIGHT LINE FORWARD. Go as fast as you can while staying in control.
  5. Crab Walk down and back x3
  6. Hunting Tiger Down and Back 3x. Walk on your forearms and feet. Stay level and as low to the ground as you can. Walk with the opposite arm and opposite leg. When stepping bring your knee all the way to your elbow.

Part Two
  1. Single Leg Squat Hold 60 Seconds - At time complete 10 Single leg jumps on the leg you squatted on. Then switch legs.
  2. Push Up Hold 60 seconds (Go down to 90 degrees)
  3. Wall Sit - 120 seconds - 10 Squat Jumps when you’re at time.
  4. Sit Up hold - 60 seconds. Rise to half way up and hold. Keep your feet flat, don’t have someone hold them. Force your core to hold them down. Keep your hands behind your head.

Repeat Part Two 3x

For the following exercises place a starting marker. Then one every 5 yards for 25 yards. (1 set = down, jog back around the outside, start again when you get back.)

  1. Squat Jump 3 sets
  2. Side Squat Jump 3 sets
  3. Single Leg Squat Jump (right leg) 3 sets
  4. Single Leg Squat Jump (left leg) 3 sets

Using the same markers:


  1. 10 Pushups, Sprint to the first marker and backwards sprint to the start, 9 pushups, Sprint to the first marker and backwards to the start, 8 push ups, so on and so forth down to 1 pushup. Take 2 minutes rest after you complete this.
  2. Sprint forward two, back one
  3. Sprint forward three, back two
  4. Side Shuffle up two, back one
  5. Side Shuffle up two, back one (face other way)
  6. Single leg hop up two, back one
  7. Single leg hop up two back one (other leg)
  8. Single leg hop, transition forwards to backwards at each marker facing the same way
  9. Single leg hop, transition forwards to backwards at each marer facing the other way

Day Five - Flash


Today everything you do should be done to your absolute maximum speed. They will be short intervals, and it will be tiring but force yourself to your maximum effort. You’re only cheating yourself if you don’t.


Each oscillating exercise should be completed in 30 seconds.


Oscillation is repetitive explosive movements back and forth. Your movement should be roughly 2-3 inches. Here is an example: Video Example


  1. Oscillating Pushups. Keep flat, drop down into a pushup and oscillate between 90 and 45 degrees.
  2. Oscillating Lunges. (Both Sides)
  3. Oscillating Squats. (From 90)
  4. Oscillating Single leg Glute Lift. If you have never done these, watch this video before your workout so you know what to do. Proper form is extremely important with these so you actually work the right muscles. Video Example
  5. Bicycle Kick Lunges. From the lunging position, keeping your chest up, and back leg down, press off with your front leg, and kick it in a peddling motion (full circle) before you land. Them immediately press again. There should be no pause. 10 reps, 3 sets each side.
  6. Bicycle Kick Lunges with a hop. Lunge Hop straight up, Then kick both legs into a full circle before you land back in the same lunge position. 10 reps, 3 sets each side.
  7. Elbow Squat. Feet shoulder width apart. Chest up, hands on your hip-flexor. Squat down and slide your hands down the front of your leg until you're squatting at 90, and your elbows are on your knees. Then stand back up. 25 reps. 3 sets.
  8. 10 minute plank. hands together, resting on your toes and forearms. Keep your body flat. You should be able to set a tennis ball in the middle of your back and not have it move.
