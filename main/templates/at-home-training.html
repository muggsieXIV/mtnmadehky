Mtn Made 2021 Training

    You get out what you put in

Training on your own time is the most valuable thing you can do as an athlete. Ice time is expensive and limited, but your driveway, sport court, garage or basement are all at your disposal (if you’re willing to put in the work). Many of the skills on ice, can be learned, practiced, and mastered off the ice. Skills like Stick-handling, Shooting, Strength and Conditioning, Balance, Coordination, and even your stride/edge work if you have rollerblades. In all your training remember these key points:

  • What you put in, is what you get out. If you practice sloppy and at half pace, you’ll only get that as your maximum ability when it comes time to execute on ice.
  • Practice doesn’t make perfect, perfect practice makes perfect. Always keep your knees bent, head and chest up, and keep a stable weight transfer when shooting. While shooting, your focus needs to be in accuracy. Power will come with strength, maturity, reps and perfected technique, but accuracy is a skillset you must develop. When practicing use targets, whether that be a shooter tutor or Gatorade bottles on old broken laces or any other creative target you can utilize. When stick handling, wear your gloves. Make sure your thumb is on the inside or your stick, and your eyes are up. Focus on the angles and efficient movements.
  • Push yourself our of your comfort zone but stay in control. While roller blading, keep your form as exaggerated as possible. Knees bent to 90, full extension and bring your foot back to center. Keep your chest up, butt down, and head up. Watch a video of a cheetah running at full speed, notice it’s head doesn’t bob up and down, but stays level.
  • Lastly, make it fun! Be creative with your training. Look for ways to alter drills and techniques without diminishing your form and technique. When doing a quick stick drill, you can do things like add an old chair to handle through, or broken sticks to flip the puck over. Find a way to keep yourself motivated through the years of training ahead of you.
