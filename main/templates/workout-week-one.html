Week One & Two

Day One - Legs Feed The Wolf

Warm-Up


You will need a 20 yard space for this warm-up. Place two markers down. You will be using these markers to complete the exersizes.


If you do not have a short breath and sweat at the end of the warm up, do it again because you didn't do it right.

  1. Run to the marker and back 4 times no breaks. Effort per down and back 50%, 65%, 75%, 100%
  2. Toe Walks - Walk on your toes to the marker and back
  3. Heel Walks - Walk on your heels there and back
  4. Knee Hugs or Knee to Chest Walks, keep your shoulder blades back and traps down.
  5. Walking Quad Stretch, press through your toes, and reach to the sky with your opposite arm, really stretch your quads.
  6. Inverted Hamstring Stretch - Start off standing upright with shoulders back and chest up & out. Lift your right foot slightly off the floor. Without changing the angle of your left knee, bend your hips and lower your torso as far as you can. As you bend over, lift up your arms straight out to your sides until they are in line with your torso. Turn your palms to face up. Return back to the starting position.
  7. Rotational Lunge - Lunge forward, keep your front knee above your heel, and twist towards your up leg. Really get a hip flexor, and back stretch.
  8. Lateral Lunge - Take a medium size step with your right leg and keep your right foot facing forward. Slowly lower your hips in line with the right knee. Getting low, keep your hips back and holding this position for one to two seconds
  9. Inchworm - Keeping your legs locked, and torso flat, plant your feet and walk out to the push up position with your hands, do two pushups then walk forward with your feet, keeping your knees straight and back flat
  10. 5 squat hops in place, sprint through the marker.

Workout


Roll through each drill with no rest. Take 120 seconds between sets to catch your breath. Complete 3 sets.

  1. 20 Squat Hops (Knees to chest or the rep doesn't count)
  2. 20 Push Ups (Down to 90, keep your body level)
  3. 20 Low Fives (A squat hop that you touch the soles of your feet together twice while in the air (2 high 5s with your feet).
  4. 30 seconds over and back single leg horizontally over a stick (old hockey stick or old broom stick).
  5. 30 seconds other leg
  6. 30 seconds over and back two legs vertically
  7. 30 seconds other leg
  8. 20 sit-ups (chest to your knee, do not have someone hold your legs and keep them on the ground the whole time. Hands behind your head).
  9. 10 V-Up and Rolls (Start on your back. Do a V-Up, touch your hands to your toes your shoulders should be off the ground when you touch. Then flatten out, roll left, do another V up, roll right, do another V up. 10 each way)
  10. 10 yard sprint, walk back.. x5 go again when you get back

Cool Down


Cool Downs prevent injury, and allow your body to recover faster. It also is a great opportunity to really work on your flexibility.


Each stretch you should hold for no less than 30 seconds.


Make sure you stretch out your Calves, Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings, Groin, Hip-Flexiors, Back, Shoulders, and Neck. Really work of your flexibility, and take long slow breathes.


It's a really good idea to drink 2 bottles of water, a gatorade, and a protein shake after any workout. Your body needs hydration, electrolytes, protein, sugars, vitamins, and carbohydrates to properly recover post workout. Nutritional timing is 30-60 minutes after any workout. Make sure you're taking care of your diet, and putting in what it needs to increase your optimum fitness.

Day Two - Speed Kills


Complete the warmup from Day One before you begin. Ladder Drills. Each exercise should be executed as fast as you possibly can, but without losing control. The only way to get better is to be out of your comfort zone. 30 seconds on, 15 seconds off. Repeat circuit 3x. 120 seconds between sets.


  1. Two feet in each
  2. Single Leg every square, both sides
  3. Two feet in, one out
  4. Backwards two feet in each
  5. Backwards two in one out
  6. Two up, one back
  7. Three up, two back
  8. Facing left, two feet in each
  9. Facing Right, two feet in each
  10. Facing Left, two up, one back
  11. Facing Right, two up, one back

Day Three - Muscle Man


Complete the warmup from Day One before you begin. Ladder Drills. Each exercise should be executed as fast as you possibly can, but without losing control. The only way to get better is to be out of your comfort zone. 30 seconds on, 15 seconds off. Repeat circuit 3x. 120 seconds between sets.


  1. Push ups - 25
  2. Dips (do from a firm platform thats at least 2 ft off the ground, dont let your butt touch) - 25
  3. Knee Slaps (pushups, when you push up jump, and slap your knees with your hands. - 10
  4. Reverse V Up. From standing, keep your feet still, walk out with your hands into a push up, then walk back. 20
  5. Left Side V Up.
  6. Right Side V Up./li>
  7. 30 seconds, Swimmers (lay on your stomach, legs and shoulders off the ground. Arms straight out, Left arm up Right leg up, Right arm down Left leg down. Then alternate as fast as you can.
  8. Shuttle Run. place a cone or shoe at the starting line, 10 yard mark, and 5 yard mark. Full sprint the near, far back to near, back to far, finish at starting line.
  9. 20 yard sprint x 5
  10. Facing Left, two up, one back
  11. Facing Right, two up, one back

End circuit, repeat 3x. 120 seconds between sets.

Day Four - Feel the burn


Complete the warmup from Day One before you begin. Ladder Drills. Push your limits. Burning or sore muscles is normal, don’t take days off, fight through it.


  1. Pushup. 10 seconds down. 10 seconds hold. 10 seconds up. 5x.
  2. Walk Outs. From a standing position, keep your feet planted, and walk yourself forward with only your arms. You want to fully extend your arms over your head. 5x
  3. Stretch Planks. Plank for 30 seconds. Alternate extending one arm and one leg (opposite sides). Do NOT let your body tilt. Stay level.
  4. Mule Kicks. Go on your hands and knees. Keep your back flat. Kick one leg as a time as far back behind you as you can. Alternate sides. 15 each leg.
  5. Glute Lifts. Lay on your back, Feet flat, knees bent to 90. Place one leg straight in the air and lift your butt up so only your shoulders and feet are on the ground. 25x each leg.
  6. High Knees. 30 seconds./li>
  7. Distance Jumps. x10. Jump as far as you possibly can. Mark where you start and land so you can try to beat each jump.
  8. Sliders. Get a plastic or paper plate, a plastic slide, socks on tile/wood floor, or a cheap plastic frisbee upside down. Practice extending your skating stride. Single Leg, 25x. Both sides.

( Repeat circuit 3x )


End circuit, repeat 3x. 120 seconds between sets.
