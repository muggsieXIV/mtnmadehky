Week Four

Day One - Double Circuit

Warm-Up


You will need a 20 yard space for this warm-up. Place two markers down. You will be using these markers to complete the exersizes.


If you do not have a short breath and sweat at the end of the warm up, do it again because you didn't do it right.


  1. Run to the marker and back 4 times no breaks. Effort per down and back 50%, 65%, 75%, 100%
  2. Toe Walks - Walk on your toes to the marker and back
  3. Heel Walks - Walk on your heels there and back
  4. Knee Hugs or Knee to Chest Walks, keep your shoulder blades back and traps down.
  5. Walking Quad Stretch, press through your toes, and reach to the sky with your opposite arm, really stretch your quads.
  6. Inverted Hamstring Stretch - Start off standing upright with shoulders back and chest up & out. Lift your right foot slightly off the floor. Without changing the angle of your left knee, bend your hips and lower your torso as far as you can. As you bend over, lift up your arms straight out to your sides until they are in line with your torso. Turn your palms to face up. Return back to the starting position.
  7. Rotational Lunge - Lunge forward, keep your front knee above your heel, and twist towards your up leg. Really get a hip flexor, and back stretch.
  8. Lateral Lunge - Take a medium size step with your right leg and keep your right foot facing forward. Slowly lower your hips in line with the right knee. Getting low, keep your hips back and holding this position for one to two seconds
  9. Inchworm - Keeping your legs locked, and torso flat, plant your feet and walk out to the push up position with your hands, do two pushups then walk forward with your feet, keeping your knees straight and back flat
  10. High Knees to the marker and back
  11. 5 squat hops in place, sprint through the marker.

Workout


This workout has two parts. Complete each part as a seperate circuit. Take 90 seconds between each circuit set, and two minutes between circuits. Complete each circuit two times. Push yourself. Take no breaks between exercises. Circuit ONE will require a 20 yard a space. Place two markers down 20 yards apart. For circuit two you will be stationary.

Part One
  1. High Knees and Back x2 (Chest up, knees all the way to your chest. Go as fast as you possibly can, then go faster.)
  2. Lunges and back x3 (Form is the most important thing here. Barely touch your knee to the ground directly below your body. Shoulder blades pulled back, Chest up.)
  3. Squat Hops and back x3 (Jump High and Long. You should get the distance of your body height at an absolute minimum. Knees to chest on your jump, when loading get your butt down to or below 90.)
  4. Side Lunge and back x3 (Face the same direction the whole time so that you alternate sides. Keep you chest up, knees to 90, and stay level. You should not be bobbing up and down.)
  5. Bounding and back 3x (Forward Lateral lunges. Go for speed but get at least 4 feet wide on each jump. Knees bent, chest up, butt down.)
  6. Sprint Down, Walk back x10

Part Two
  1. 50 Push ups - Get to or below 90 or it does not count. Also reach fully to the top or the rep does not count.
  2. 50 Sit ups - Do NOT have someone hold your feet. You must be able to keep your feet flat while doing a sit up. If not, your abs are too weak. Also, keep your arms crossed, your shoulder blades must touch the ground, and your elbows while flat to your torso shsould touch your knees.
  3. 50 Squat hops - Down below 90, keep your chest up, explode as high as you can. Do not sacrafice form for fatigue or burn. Fight through and hold your form the entire time. Bad form reps DO NOT COUNT.
  4. 50 Single leg squats (each leg). Do not use a box or wall to balance. Free stand, balance, and keep your non-using foot off the ground. Keep your chest up, butt down, and shoulder blades pulled back. Squat to 90. For these ones, I do not want Piston Squats, keep your leg behind you like a skating position.
  5. 50 Glute Lifts - Shoulder blades flat on the ground. Press your hips straight into the air to full extension.
  6. 50 Heel Touching Squats - Keep your feet shoulder width apart. Knees and Toes forward.

Cool Down


Cool Downs prevent injury, and allow your body to recover faster. It also is a great opportunity to really work on your flexibility.


Each stretch you should hold for no less than 30 seconds.


Make sure you stretch out your Calves, Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings, Groin, Hip-Flexiors, Back, Shoulders, and Neck. Really work of your flexibility, and take long slow breathes.


It's a really good idea to drink 2 bottles of water, a gatorade, and a protein shake after any workout. Your body needs hydration, electrolytes, protein, sugars, vitamins, and carbohydrates to properly recover post workout. Nutritional timing is 30-60 minutes after any workout. Make sure you're taking care of your diet, and putting in what it needs to increase your optimum fitness.

Week Four

Day Two - Welcome to the Jungle


This workout is designed to build coordination and athleticism. The First few exercises should feel very awkward and uncomfortable. Place two markers 20 yards apart for this circuit.


Part One
  1. Monkey Runs Down and Back 3x (Face the same way). Keep your chest up, knees bent to 90, arms above your head like a monkey. You’re going to forward/lateral Cross Over. No steps in between, just constant one way cross overs through the line. Do this as fast as you can! Heres a video of a monkey if you don’t understand my description: Monkey Walk
  2. Bear Crawl Down and Back 3x. Get on all fours and run! as fast as you can.
  3. Scorpions 20x each way. Lay on your stomach with your arms stretched out straight from your shoulders. Bring your opposite leg to opposite hand while keeping your other leg straight, and your arms/shoulders down. Do these with pace but with control.
  4. Duck Walks Down and Back 3x. Chest up, Butt to heels (it should touch lightly), Walk forward, DO NOT BRING YOUR LEGS TO THE SIDE KEEP THEM MOVING IN A STRAIGHT LINE FORWARD. Go as fast as you can while staying in control.
  5. Crab Walk down and back x3
  6. Hunting Tiger Down and Back 3x. Walk on your forearms and feet. Stay level and as low to the ground as you can. Walk with the opposite arm and opposite leg. When stepping bring your knee all the way to your elbow.

Part Two
  1. Single Leg Squat Hold 60 Seconds - At time complete 10 Single leg jumps on the leg you squatted on. Then switch legs.
  2. Push Up Hold 60 seconds (Go down to 90 degrees)
  3. Wall Sit - 120 seconds - 10 Squat Jumps when you’re at time.
  4. Sit Up hold - 60 seconds. Rise to half way up and hold. Keep your feet flat, don’t have someone hold them. Force your core to hold them down. Keep your hands behind your head.

Repeat Part Two 3x

Day Three - Formula 1


Circuit One

Get a strong warm up in. Place markers in a diamond 10 yards apart from one another. Complete each exercise 5 times each, the 5 times the other direction. On #6, shuffle through the middle as drawn. Take 15-20 seconds between runs.



Circuit Two

Record your time on each sprint. Beat your scores.


  1. 5 yard sprint 5x
  2. 10 yard sprint 5x
  3. 15 yard spring 5x
  4. 20 yard sprint 5x
  5. 15 to 10 to 20 5x
  6. 5 yard sprint 5x

Stretch and Recover

Week Four

Day Four - Challenge Day


Today we’re going to find your limits. Get a good warm up, and prepare to put your max into each exercise.


  1. Squat Jumps. Jump for Distance. Record where you land. You must stick the jump for it to count. Complete 15 jumps.
  2. Single Leg Jumps. Jump and land from the same leg. You must stick it to count. Try to beat your jumps. 15 each leg.
  3. Lateral Side Jumps. Jump from one leg, land on the other. Face directly sideways and jump straight to your side. Mark your jumps, try to beat them. 15 Jumps each leg.
  4. Speed Push Ups. Do as many pushups as you possibly can in 20 seconds. Have someone count reps and track time. 5 sets. Try to beat each set. No more than 60 seconds between each set. if you do not go all the way to 90 and all the way to full extension at the top, the rep does not count.
  5. Speed Sit Ups. Do as many as possible in 20 seconds. Have someone count reps and track time. 5 sets. Try to beat each set. No more than 60 seconds between sets. If you do not come all the way knees to chest and shoulders to the floor your rep does not count.
  6. Wall Sit. Two sets. Take 2 minutes between sets. Go to your absolute max. Your first set should be a minimum 10 minutes, your second should not be less than 8 minutes.

Stetch and Recover

Week Four

Day Five - Finish Strong


Complete the warmup from Day One before you begin. Place markers at 5, 10, and 20 yards.


  1. 5 yard sprint, walk back x3
  2. 10 yard sprint, walk back x3
  3. 20 yard sprint, walk back x3
  4. Quick Stick Hops (Hop front to back over a stick as fast as you can. 30 seconds, then Vertically for 30 seconds.)
  5. Lunges with a hop, 20 yards down and back.
  6. Squat Hops, 20 yards down and back.
  7. 50 Push Ups
  8. 50 Sit Ups
  9. Duck Walk, 20 yards down and back
  10. Bear Crawl, 20 yards down and back

Stretch and Recover


Post Circuit Wall sit challenge. Try to hit 10 minutes. Keep your back flat, knees bend to 90. Hands off your legs.
